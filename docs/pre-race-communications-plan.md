# Pre-Race Communications Plan

## Purpose

This document defines the communication sequence from registration launch through race week.

## Assumptions

- event date: `May 2, 2026`
- car cap: `60`
- registration is opening soon through Square
- cars are built at home from included kits
- the event wants to feel welcoming, local, and community-first

## Communication Goals

- make the event easy to understand
- convert attention into ticket purchases
- reduce confusion about what the ticket includes
- remind people to actually build the car before race day
- reduce check-in friction by setting expectations early

## Core Message To Repeat

`Each $35 ticket includes one car kit and one race entry. Build at home and race together on May 2.`

## Suggested Campaign Sequence

### 1. Registration Launch

When:

- as soon as the Square page is ready

Purpose:

- announce the event
- explain what a ticket includes
- send people directly to registration

Key points:

- one ticket = one car entry
- one kit included per ticket
- participants may buy more than one entry
- all kinds of families, grandparents, friends, and adults are welcome
- limited to `60` cars

### 2. Early Momentum Reminder

When:

- roughly 7 to 10 days after launch

Purpose:

- keep the event visible
- create mild urgency without sounding pushy

Key points:

- tickets are available now
- cars are built at home
- this is for more than just current scout families

### 3. Build-Phase Resource Reminder

When:

- about 2 to 3 weeks before race day

Purpose:

- help registered participants stay on track
- remind undecided people that resources exist

Key points:

- build resources are available
- replacement links exist if needed
- embellishment and design creativity are welcome within the rules

### 4. Race-Week Reminder

When:

- week of the event

Purpose:

- reduce check-in confusion
- remind participants to finish the car

Key points:

- bring the car fully assembled
- all cars may race
- only inspection-compliant cars are eligible for speed trophies
- all cars remain eligible for design recognition
- include arrival and check-in details once finalized

### 5. Day-Before Reminder

When:

- one day before race day

Purpose:

- reduce no-shows
- make arrival easy

Key points:

- location
- arrival time
- what to bring
- where to go first

## Channel Plan By Message Type

### Public Awareness

Use:

- Facebook
- Peachjar
- flyers and posters
- storefront partners
- community boards

### Registered Participant Messaging

Use:

- Square confirmation flow
- direct email if available

## Suggested Public Copy Themes

### Launch Theme

`Build a car at home and race it together at the Community Gravity Car Derby.`

### Broad Welcome Theme

`Families, grandparents, friends, and adults are all welcome.`

### Urgency Theme

`Entries are limited to 60 cars.`

### Build Help Theme

`Need help getting started? Build resources and replacement links are available.`

## Information Every Registration Page Should Include

- event name
- date
- location
- price
- what the ticket includes
- car cap
- who is welcome
- basic build-at-home expectation
- link to resources

## Known Open Fields To Fill Later

- exact registration open date
- exact registration close date
- exact check-in window
- late-arrival policy wording
- final tagline selection
