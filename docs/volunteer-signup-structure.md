# Volunteer Signup Structure

## Purpose

This document defines the recommended signup form structure for the Community Gravity Car Derby. It is designed to:

- let people volunteer even if they only have 1 to 3 hours
- separate race-control roles from guest-facing roles
- identify async helpers who can contribute before race day
- collect only the information needed to place people quickly

## Signup Strategy

Use one form with four parts:

1. basic contact information
2. availability and time blocks
3. role preferences by category
4. skills, limits, and notes

Do not ask volunteers to choose from a long list of vague titles without context. Group roles by type of work instead.

## Recommended Form Intro

Use text like this at the top of the form:

`We are looking for both race-day volunteers and pre-event helpers. You do not need to know how everything works ahead of time. Choose the types of jobs that fit you best, tell us when you are available, and we will match you where you can help most.`

## Section 1: Contact Information

Fields:

- full name
- email
- phone number
- best way to contact you

Optional:

- organization, pack, troop, or family connection

## Section 2: Availability

Ask volunteers to select every time block they can cover.

Options:

- `Day Before Setup` Friday 3:00 PM to 7:00 PM
- `Event Morning` Saturday 8:00 AM to opening ceremony
- `Race Block` opening ceremony through finals
- `Awards / Teardown` awards through closeout
- `Async Help Before Event` work that can be done before race day

Then ask:

- `How much time can you realistically give?`

Options:

- under 1 hour
- 1 to 2 hours
- 2 to 3 hours
- half day
- most of the event

## Section 3: Role Preference Categories

Ask volunteers to choose up to three categories they would be happy to help with.

Options:

- `Check-In and Inspection`
  - greeting racers
  - moving families through check-in
  - helping with inspection steps
- `Pit and Repair`
  - helping with minor car fixes
  - supporting reinspection flow
- `Race-Floor Operations`
  - staging
  - finish and retrieval
  - runner or floater roles
- `Guest Experience and Recruiting`
  - welcome
  - recruiting table
  - hospitality
  - design-award support
- `Setup and Teardown`
  - tables
  - chairs
  - signs
  - equipment movement
- `Async Help Before Event`
  - communications
  - printing
  - awards production
  - signage and prep materials

## Section 4: Specific Role Interests

Use a checklist and allow multiple selections.

### Race-Day Options

- Check-in support
- Inspection assistant
- Pit / repair support
- Staging runner
- Finish / retrieval
- Floater
- Greeter / welcome
- Recruiting support
- Hospitality / concessions
- Design-award support
- Setup crew
- Teardown crew

### Async Options

- Communications helper
- Printing coordinator
- Awards production helper
- Signage and materials prep
- Recruiting materials helper

## Section 5: Skills And Comfort

Use checkbox or short-answer fields for matching.

Ask:

- `What are you comfortable with?`

Options:

- greeting people
- following a checklist
- working with children and families
- repeated process tasks
- basic computer work
- lifting and moving equipment
- making minor repairs
- organizing supplies
- making signs or printed materials
- writing or editing messages

Ask:

- `What should we avoid assigning you to?`

Short answer.

## Section 6: Training And Constraints

Ask:

- `Would you be willing to take a short briefing before your shift?`
  - yes
  - no

- `Do you have any physical limitations or scheduling constraints we should know about?`

Short answer.

## Section 7: Leadership Interest

Use only if you still need leads.

Ask:

- `Would you be willing to be contacted about a lead role if we need one?`
  - yes
  - no

## Recommended Matching Rules

- assign critical leads outside the public signup form whenever possible
- use the form mainly for support roles and async help
- place detail-oriented people into check-in and inspection
- place guest-facing and creative people into welcome, recruiting, hospitality, and design-award support
- place technically steady people into race-floor support
- place low-availability volunteers into async tasks or short-shift support

## Minimum Data Needed To Place A Volunteer

If the form needs to stay very short, keep only:

- name
- email or phone
- time blocks available
- preferred role categories
- one question about skills or limits

## Suggested Follow-Up Workflow

After a person signs up:

1. confirm receipt
2. assign a tentative role and time block
3. send a short role-specific expectation note
4. confirm where to report and who they report to
