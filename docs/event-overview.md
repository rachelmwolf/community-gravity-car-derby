# Event Overview

## Intent

The Community Gravity Car Derby is designed to extend the pinewood-derby-style family experience to a broader community audience while reflecting the quality, discipline, and values of the Pack 110 program.

Year one is a pilot. Success is defined by:

- a smooth and fair event
- a strong participant experience
- manageable volunteer workload
- no major safety or inspection failures
- documentation strong enough to repeat the event next year

## Known Event Details

- Event dates: May 1-2, 2026
- Day-before window: 3:00 PM to 7:00 PM
- Event-day window: 8:00 AM to 3:00 PM
- Assumed field size in the current planner: 60 cars

## Planning Goals

- create a repeatable operating model
- make volunteer roles clear and attractive
- protect race fairness and pacing
- maintain a welcoming family-facing atmosphere
- make scouting resources available without letting recruiting dominate the room
- consider goodwill and access policies such as volunteer discounts or reduced-fee entries
- support future organizers outside the current council context

## Risks Already Identified

- volunteer no-shows or role confusion
- check-in congestion and late arrivals
- inspection disputes or fairness concerns
- race day running long
- low community turnout in year one
- participant experience inconsistency

## Planning Stance

The event should borrow its family-facing format from strong pinewood derby events and borrow its execution discipline from formal gravity or soap box races. That means:

- clear written rules
- deliberate inspection standards
- controlled pit and staging flow
- named decision-makers
- volunteer briefing before race activity
- visible guest communication throughout the event
