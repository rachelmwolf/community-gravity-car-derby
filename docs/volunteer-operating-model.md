# Volunteer Operating Model

## Why The Current Volunteer Tab Needs To Change

The current planner lists several reasonable roles, but it is still too title-driven for a first-year volunteer-only event. For this event, the right model is:

- start with functions that must be covered
- define what kind of volunteer fits each function
- mark which roles require continuity or judgment
- let volunteers choose based on fit and preference
- assign backups for every critical function

This makes the staffing model more resilient and easier to reuse.

## Core Command Structure

These roles should be assigned early and filled by reliable, decision-capable people.

### Race Director

- Owns event execution, timing, and final day-of decisions
- Best fit: calm decision-maker who can hold the whole event picture
- Coverage: full event lifecycle
- Backup needed: yes

### Chief Inspector

- Owns inspection standards, rule interpretation, and pass/fail decisions
- Best fit: detail-oriented, fair, comfortable enforcing standards
- Coverage: pre-event prep, check-in, early event day
- Backup needed: yes

### Safety Lead

- Owns first-aid readiness, emergency response path, hazard scanning, and escalation
- Best fit: safety-minded volunteer, medical background preferred but not required if an event safety plan exists
- Coverage: setup through teardown
- Backup needed: yes

## Core Operations Roles

### Event Host / MC

- Keeps energy up, makes announcements, explains flow, supports awards
- Best fit: outgoing, comfortable on mic, can stay composed under schedule changes
- Coverage: opening through awards

### Check-In Lead

- Owns participant intake, arrival flow, numbering, waiver/material handoff, and queue control
- Best fit: organized, guest-friendly, good with lines and paperwork
- Coverage: pre-open through end of check-in

### Inspection Team

- Verifies cars against rules and inspection checklist
- Best fit: patient, consistent, calm under minor disputes
- Coverage: check-in period and any approved reinspection

### Pit / Repair Lead

- Manages fix-it area for cars that need adjustments before acceptance
- Best fit: handy, resourceful, comfortable helping families under time pressure
- Coverage: active during check-in and inspection

### Staging Lead

- Controls accepted cars after inspection, maintains race-ready order, keeps heats fed
- Best fit: orderly, assertive, good at repetitive flow control
- Coverage: race period

### Track Operations / Starter

- Runs heats, coordinates start process, protects pacing
- Best fit: technically confident, consistent, not easily distracted
- Coverage: race period

### Scoring / Results

- Operates software or result tracking, validates outputs, supports awards data
- Best fit: detail-oriented, comfortable with computers, steady under time pressure
- Coverage: race period through awards prep

### Finish / Retrieval Support

- Returns cars, resets lanes, keeps transition time low
- Best fit: mobile, dependable, comfortable with repetitive movement
- Coverage: race period

## Family-Facing Support Roles

### Greeter / Welcome

- Welcomes families, answers basic questions, directs them to the correct area
- Best fit: friendly, approachable, likes guest interaction

### Floater

- Covers breaks, fills gaps, runs messages, solves small operational bottlenecks
- Best fit: flexible, observant, willing to do whatever is needed

### Hospitality / Recruiting

- Runs food, information, and community-facing engagement functions
- Best fit: guest-facing volunteer who does not need to be embedded in race control

## Staffing Tiers

These counts are planning targets inferred from comparable events and should be refined after expected attendance is known.

### Lean Event: 25 To 40 Cars

- 1 Race Director
- 1 Chief Inspector
- 1 Safety Lead
- 1 MC
- 1 Check-In Lead plus 1 support volunteer
- 1 Pit / Repair Lead
- 1 Staging Lead
- 1 Track Ops / Starter
- 1 Scoring volunteer
- 1 Floater
- 3 to 4 setup / teardown volunteers

### Standard Event: 40 To 80 Cars

- all lean roles, plus:
- 1 additional inspection volunteer
- 1 additional check-in support volunteer
- 1 finish / retrieval volunteer
- 1 hospitality / recruiting volunteer
- 1 additional floater or runner

### Expanded Event: 80+ Cars Or Higher Production Ambition

- all standard roles, plus:
- separate awards support
- additional staging support
- additional family-flow support
- separate volunteer coordinator
- more formal safety and communications support

## Role Design Standard

Every volunteer posting or assignment sheet should answer:

- why this role matters
- what the volunteer will actually do
- what type of person fits the role
- what shift length and time window apply
- what training or briefing will be provided
- what issues the volunteer can solve alone
- what must be escalated

## Immediate Implication For The Current Planner

The existing `Volunteers` tab should be reworked into:

1. critical leads
2. operational roles
3. guest-facing support roles
4. setup / teardown roles
5. backup coverage
6. role-fit notes

That structure will be easier to staff and easier to reuse next year.
