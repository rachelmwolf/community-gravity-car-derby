# Registration Workflow

## Purpose

Use this as the working flow for participant registration and race-day check-in.

## Registration Goals

- families know exactly how to register
- families know what to bring and when to arrive
- check-in runs quickly
- inspection starts with good information

## Registration Flow

### Before Event Day

1. Open registration with a clear deadline.
2. Collect:
   - participant name
   - parent / guardian name
   - contact email
   - contact phone
   - car name
   - any category or age-group information if used
3. Send confirmation message after registration.
4. Send reminder message before the event.
5. If registration happens late in the cycle, send a catch-up message with the essential build and race information.

## Participant Instructions To Communicate

- bring the completed car to check-in
- arrive early enough for inspection
- all cars may race
- only inspection-compliant cars are eligible for speed trophies
- all cars remain eligible for design recognition
- bring the car fully assembled and race-ready

## Event-Day Check-In Flow

1. Family arrives at check-in table.
2. Volunteer confirms participant information.
3. Car number and any check-in materials are assigned.
4. Family is directed immediately to inspection.
5. If the car passes inspection, it goes to impound / staging.
6. If the car needs a fix, the family goes to pit / repair and returns for reinspection.

## Late Arrival Policy

Working recommendation:

- publish a firm check-in close time
- cars arriving after check-in closes may race only if they can be processed without disrupting the event flow
- no late car should delay opening or scheduled racing

Final policy should be confirmed before participant messaging is sent.

## Open Decision

- whether to use staggered check-in windows or one general arrival window

## Organizer Checklist

- registration owner assigned
- registration form live
- confirmation message drafted
- catch-up message drafted for late registrants
- reminder message drafted
- check-in close time decided
- late-arrival policy decided
- check-in table materials prepared

## Access And Discount Policy

This event may choose to offer limited goodwill or access support, but it should be decided intentionally before registration opens.

Possible options to evaluate:

- one reduced-fee entry for a volunteer family
- reduced-fee or sponsored entries for underserved families
- committee-approved discount codes
- sponsor-funded community entries

If any of these are used, the committee should define:

- who qualifies
- who approves it
- how many discounted entries are available
- how it is tracked in registration
- how it is communicated without creating confusion or pressure
