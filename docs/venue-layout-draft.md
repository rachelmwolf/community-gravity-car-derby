# Venue Layout Draft

## Working Assumptions

- venue is a gym / auditorium space
- usable floor area is about one basketball court plus roughly 5 feet around it
- stage runs along the north long side
- stage length is about 40 feet
- track length is about 40 feet
- family event entry is through the north-side gym doors
- there are two gym-entry doors for participants and families
- check-in happens at the gym doors
- inspection runs along the east wall
- all race activity and awards happen in front of the stage
- church provides about 200 chairs and 20 six-foot tables
- concessions and recruiting are both expected
- two smart TVs, one projector, and one large projection screen are available

## Draft Zone Layout

### North Side

- stage along the north wall
- awards area centered in front of stage
- track set parallel to the stage but offset enough to leave a volunteer access lane behind or beside the track
- smart TV or projector display visible from the audience

### North Entry Area

- welcome and check-in at the two north-side gym doors
- clear signage from the south/main building entrance directing families to the gym entry
- one table for welcome / queue guidance
- one or more tables for check-in

### East Wall

- inspection station along the east wall
- pit / repair immediately adjacent to inspection
- impound / staging near inspection but controlled so passed cars move toward the track efficiently

### Center Floor

- family seating spread across the center and west portions of the gym
- leave a closer viewing lane near the track for racers and active families
- do not place the general audience so close that they interfere with track operations

### West Side

- overflow seating
- possible concessions or recruiting if that keeps the track side less congested

## Recommended Functional Placement

| Zone | Location | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Welcome / check-in | north doors | catches families immediately |
| Inspection | east wall | keeps technical flow off the main audience path |
| Pit / repair | east wall beside inspection | supports quick fixes and reinspection |
| Impound / staging | east side moving toward track | reduces backtracking |
| Track | north side in front of stage | keeps racing and awards in one visual focal area |
| Scoring / software | near track with outlet and display access | supports Derbynet and race control |
| Awards | in front of stage | clear presentation focal point |
| Concessions | away from check-in and inspection lines | keeps food traffic from colliding with operations |
| Recruiting | near concessions or audience flow, but not at check-in choke point | good visibility without slowing registration |
| First aid | visible but off the main track path | easy access without crowding operations |

## Audience Plan

- spread families across seating rather than concentrating them tightly at the track
- create a closer-viewing area for current racers and active participants
- keep the rest of the audience slightly farther back to reduce crowding at the track

## Setup Priorities

1. mark track footprint in front of stage
2. mark check-in at north doors
3. mark inspection and pit area on east wall
4. mark impound / staging path from inspection toward track
5. place scoring / software near track and display equipment
6. place audience seating with a racer viewing lane near track
7. place awards zone in front of stage
8. place concessions and recruiting outside the main check-in / inspection flow
